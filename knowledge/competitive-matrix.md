## Strategic Notes

**ServiceNow acquiring Veza (Dec 2025)** is the single most important competitive event. Veza's Access Graph inside ServiceNow's workflow engine + ITSM installed base = the "good enough" identity security for enterprises already on ServiceNow. This compresses the window for startups to land before the integrated offering ships (likely late 2026/2027).

**ConductorOne's speed wedge** is real. <4 weeks to go-live, 300+ OOTB connectors, open-source Baton framework. Winning on "replace legacy IGA without a 2-year implementation." Linx should study their connector strategy.

**Lumos's agentic bet** (Albus multi-agent system Jun 2025 → Agentic UARs Dec 2025 → Identity Security Agents Mar 2026) is the most ambitious AI play. Closed loop: discover → reason → remediate → codify as policy. Question: can they execute where data is messy?

**"AI-native" is crowded.** ConductorOne, Lumos, and Veza all claim it. Differentiation isn't "we use AI" — it's measurable toil elimination. Linx should avoid the AI messaging arms race and focus on outcomes.
